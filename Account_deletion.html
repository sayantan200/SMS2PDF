Sms2Pdf Account Deletion

Effective Date: May 26, 2026

This page explains how users of Sms2Pdf can request deletion of their account and associated app data.

App Name: Sms2Pdf
Package ID: com.sms2pdf.app
Developer Contact: psayantan08@gmail.com

How to Request Account Deletion

To request deletion of your Sms2Pdf account, please send an email to:

psayantan08@gmail.com

Please include the following information in your email:

  • Subject: Delete My Sms2Pdf Account
  • The email address used to sign in to Sms2Pdf
  • Your request to delete your account and associated app data

For security, please send the request from the same email address that you used to sign in to the app whenever possible.

What Data Will Be Deleted

After verifying your request, we will delete or anonymize account-related data stored by Sms2Pdf, including:

  • Your Firebase user account record where applicable
  • Your Sms2Pdf user profile data, such as name, email, profile photo URL, and user ID
  • Stored app metadata linked to your account
  • Referral-related data linked to your account
  • Subscription entitlement metadata stored in the app database
  • SMS, receipt, invoice, or PDF metadata stored in Firebase and linked to your account, if present

Data Not Deleted Automatically

Sms2Pdf may save generated PDF files to your own Google Drive when you use Google Drive integration. Files stored in your Google Drive are controlled by your Google account and are not automatically deleted by Sms2Pdf.

To delete those files, open your Google Drive and remove the files or folders created by Sms2Pdf, such as the Sms2Pdf folder.

Subscriptions

Deleting your Sms2Pdf account does not automatically cancel an active Google Play subscription. To cancel a subscription, open the Google Play Store, go to Payments & subscriptions, and cancel the Sms2Pdf subscription there.

Retention Period

Account deletion requests are normally processed within 30 days. Some limited information may be retained for a longer period if required for legal, tax, fraud prevention, dispute resolution, security, or payment compliance purposes.

Local Device Data

Some app data may be stored locally on your device, such as preferences, cached receipt entries, generated files, and settings. You can remove local device data by uninstalling the app or clearing the app's storage from your device settings.
